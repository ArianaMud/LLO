Llyn Llech Owain

What is LLyn Llech Owain?

It's a shallow lake that is 8.6 acres in area. However the lake is primarily known and regonised from its welsh legend and beautiful wildlife. The word "Llyn Llech Owain" translates from Welsh to "the lake of Owain's slab", this relates to the folk legend that Owain was commanded to look after a well named Mynydd Mawr. Every day Owain would take a bit of water enough to sustain him and his horse but remembering to replace it. One fateful day he forget this important act resulting in the creation of the lake.

What Wildlife is there?

  • Hazel dormouse
  • Green Woodpecker
  • Eurasian Jay
  • Willow tit
  • Goldcrest
  • Buzzards
